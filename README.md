# Philosophy Writing

Selected long-form philosophy writing from my BA Philosophy.

These pieces are included as part of my wider portfolio because philosophy shaped the way I think: questioning assumptions, analysing complex systems, working with uncertainty, and trying to make difficult ideas clear.

My current work is focused on data science, analytics, and data engineering, but this writing shows the intellectual background that led me there. Before I became interested in data, I was already interested in interpretation, evidence, power, meaning, and how people construct systems of belief.

## Included writing

### 1. The Uyghur as *Homo Sacer*

An analysis of the treatment of Uyghurs in Xinjiang through Michel Foucault’s theory of biopolitics and Giorgio Agamben’s concepts of *homo sacer*, bare life, the camp, and the state of exception.

The piece examines how political power can classify a population as a threat, remove normal legal protections, and produce spaces where exclusion becomes normalised.

**Themes:**  
Biopolitics, sovereignty, state of exception, bare life, modern racism, human rights, political violence.

### 2. The Great Awakening of QAnon’s *Weltanschauung*

A psychoanalytic interpretation of QAnon and conspiracy theory in an age of post-truth.

The piece uses Roland Barthes, Jacques Lacan, and Slavoj Žižek to analyse how online conspiracy movements construct meaning, distrust authority, and create paranoid systems of belief around hidden power.

**Themes:**  
Psychoanalysis, conspiracy theory, post-truth, Lacan, Barthes, Žižek, paranoia, symbolic efficiency, online belief systems.
